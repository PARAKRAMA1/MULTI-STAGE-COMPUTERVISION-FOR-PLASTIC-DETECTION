This study presents a novel Resilient, Hierarchical Remote Sensing (RHRS)
framework to overcome limitations of scale, environmental noise, and inaccu-
rate quantification in current aquatic plastic debris monitoring. Traditional
methods face a trade-off between low-resolution satellite imagery and noise-
sensitive, limited-coverage drone vision systems. The RHRS framework employs
a multi-stage, coarse-to-fine detection pipeline. Stage 1 uses a machine learn-
ing model on Sentinel-2 multispectral data to identify macroplastic hotspots
over large areas. Stage 2 triggers a low-power YOLOv8-based object detection
model on UAVs for high-resolution local analysis. A key innovation is training
the vision model with synthetic data augmentation to mitigate environmental
variabilities such as sun glare, water turbidity, and surface ripples, achieving
high robustness in real-world conditions. The framework also integrates an
Occlusion- Aware DeepSORT tracking algorithm, enabling unique counting
of debris beyond simple detection. Validation demonstrates a mean Average
Precision (mAP) ¿90conditions and counting accuracy ¿95of plastic mass flux
(kg/hr) for informed policy decisions and targeted environmental interventions.
